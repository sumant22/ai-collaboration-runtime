## REVIEW

### Summary
The response provides a practical and easy-to-follow customer follow-up system
suitable for a small retail shop with limited resources. It aligns closely with
the stated constraints by using free tools, maintaining low operational complexity,
and keeping daily maintenance within the required time limit.

### Findings

1. The proposed workflow directly addresses the problem of forgotten follow-ups with a centralized tracking process.
2. The use of Google Sheets and WhatsApp is appropriate for a low-cost, low-technical-overhead environment.
3. The phased implementation structure improves clarity and makes adoption easier for staff.
4. The suggested tracker fields are sufficient for a basic sales follow-up workflow.
5. The daily routine is realistic and fits within the 10–15 minute operational constraint.
6. The follow-up message template provides a usable starting point for consistent communication.
7. The recommendation to switch to HubSpot Free CRM introduces unnecessary future complexity outside the requested simplicity-first approach.
8. The notebook backup option is mentioned but lacks a concrete operational process or example layout.
9. The response does not define how overdue follow-ups should be identified or escalated.
10. Ownership responsibilities between owner and staff are implied but not explicitly defined.

### Risks

1. Staff may forget to update the sheet consistently over time.
2. Duplicate entries could occur from multiple inquiry channels.
3. Without prioritization rules, important customers may still be missed.
4. Reliance on manual discipline creates risk of process degradation.
5. Google Sheets usability on mobile devices may become difficult if inquiry volume increases.

### Recommendation
Needs Revision

---

## VERIFICATION

### Verified Areas

1. Solution uses only free tools permitted by the task constraints.
2. Workflow is simple enough for non-technical staff.
3. Daily process remains within the 15-minute maintenance limit.
4. System covers all inquiry channels mentioned in the task.
5. Response provides actionable implementation steps rather than abstract advice.

### Concerns

1. HubSpot CRM recommendation conflicts with the simplicity-focused requirement.
2. No detailed notebook fallback workflow is provided.
3. No prioritization mechanism for urgent or high-value leads.
4. Overdue follow-ups are not operationally handled.
5. Process depends heavily on owner oversight for consistency.

### Missing Information

1. Example tracker row or sample sheet structure.
2. Simple paper notebook format for offline use.
3. Clear rules for follow-up timing and stopping conditions.
4. Defined ownership responsibilities between staff and owner.
5. Basic prioritization system (e.g., urgent, hot lead, pending).

### Confidence Level
High — the review is based on direct alignment checks against the task
objectives, operational constraints, and practical usability requirements.
